About

Why OpenMM-Arena?

A unified, navigable taxonomy for the rapidly converging landscape of multimodal artificial intelligence.

The field of multimodal artificial intelligence has witnessed an unprecedented convergence of historically independent research traditions. Text-to-image synthesis and text-to-video generation have matured from niche subfields into core pillars of generative AI, while image-to-video generation bridges static imagery with temporal dynamics through learned motion priors. 3D vision — powered by Neural Radiance Fields, 3D Gaussian Splatting, LLM-driven understanding, and SLAM — extends generative AI into the spatial domain, connecting these representations to real-time robotics, navigation, and embodied agents. 4D spatial intelligence adds the temporal dimension through video depth estimation, dense 3D tracking, dynamic scene reconstruction, human-centric motion capture, and physics-based simulation. These intersect deeply with world models that seek to capture environment dynamics, and unified architectures that dissolve the boundary between visual understanding and generation. OpenMM-Arena serves as the definitive, centralized knowledge base that organizes, maps, bridges, and catalogues this expansive literature.
